## Introduction

The purpose of this document is to provide a Quickfire high-level overview of the regulations for Greece regulated market.

This document should not be regarded as an exhausted list of all requirements and shall not be used for development purposes or as a communication deliverable sent to an operator.

Detailed Business Requirement Documents and Operator guidelines will be prepared for that purpose upon project go ahead & initiation.

## Operations

The Greek State has exclusive jurisdiction for conducting online gaming on Greek territory and this jurisdiction is exercised through specially licensed providers.

The terms and conditions must mention that only players who are legally permitted by the Authority with the relevant jurisdiction can participate in games of chance (i.e. Only players in Greece can participate in games)

The interactive gaming system and/or the player system must be able to detect the physical location of an authorized player who attempts to access the service and must not allow betting if the player is in an area where this type of gaming is prohibited (No player can participate outside of Greece)

Gaming shall be conducted via websites whose domain name must end in “.gr”, therefore, access to the operator’s services in Greece, through the Gateway, may only be achieved via websites ending in “.gr”. The operator must host the Gateway inside the EU or the EEA.

HGC aims at minimizing Player exposure to security risks from the systems/subsystems and software of online gaming. The License Holder must hold an ISO/EIC 27001 certification.

## Licenses

The operator will need a license to operate in Greece. There are two types of licenses.

- Betting License (Type 1)
- License for Other Online Games (Type 2)

The consideration for a Type 2 License is set at EUR 2,000,000.

Each License is valid for seven (7) years from the date it was issued.

The license holder may submit a request to renew the License for the same period of time to HGC, at least one year before the License expires.

The person/s applying for a license must be legal persons with registered seat or permanent establishment in Greece or another Member State of the EU

**Note:** Type 1 refers to Sportsbook hence N/A for QF. Type 2 license is only required.

## TAX

For all games of chance, participation of the Greek State in the revenues is set at thirty percent (30%) of the gross profit regarding amounts generated from the license holder's activity.

The rate of the Greek State's participation in the gross profit from games may increase up to five percentage points, reaching a maximum of thirty five percent (35%).

## Registration

Operators would need to provide a compliant registration solution.

Multiple accounts per player are not allowed.

Players must be > 21 years of age.

Players cannot create a new account within 12 months of closing their existing account.

## Currency

There are no specific currency requirements in the legislation, however the Euro is mentioned in the legislation where amounts are listed. Greece is a member of the EU and the Euro is the official currency of Greece. As a result, the assumption is that the Euro will be the default currency.

## Languages

There is no mention of the language requirements in the legislation.

**Note:** We can assume that English only, semi translated and fully translated games in Greek may be allowed. We need to confirm with the regulator.

## Games

The name of the game that the participant is playing must be clearly visible on all associated screens.

The game must clearly show to the player the range of the amount of a potential win, the multiplier’s range etc. that may be won in the bonus game.

The player must be given a choice on whether or not he wants to participate in gamble features (for applicable games). Any unusual game conditions during which the gamble feature is not available, must be explained.

## RNG

The **Random Number Generator (RNG)** and technical means ensure that the results of card games are random.

The **RNG** must be continuously cycled in the background between games and during one speed games, while said speed cannot be timed by the player.

The first seed must be randomly determined by an uncontrolled event. After each game, there must be a random change in the **RNG** process (new seed, random timer, delay etc.).

The **RNG** must be certified.

**Note:** We need to confirm if these **RNG** requirements are similar to Lithuania where a Land Based **RNG** is required. Big concerns if so.

## Autoplay

Autoplay is not mentioned in the regulations hence we assume Autoplay is optional.

Autoplay must be stopped at any given time, if enabled.

## RTP

The **RTP** must be displayed in the help files.

The minimum payout in games of chance is set at eighty percent (80%)

## Card Games

Card games must comply with the following:

- Games that use multiple decks of cards must clearly state the number of cards and the number of card decks in play.
- When the cards are removed from the deck, they must not be put back, unless the rules of the game allow it.
- The deck must not be re-shuffled, unless the rules of the game allow it.

## Games Rules

Games rules must be made available to the player on the operator’s website. No language requirement is listed. We would need to provide game help files for all Microgaming games.

The rules must:

a. contain the **RTP**

b. Inform the player on how funds are returned for games incomplete games cancelled by the operator.

c. state the payments of the prizes when multiple victories are possible.

## Playcheck

The License Holder provides the Player with access to information regarding the balance of his Electronic Account, the history of Playing Activity, including Participation, profits and losses, deposits and withdrawals and other relevant transactions. This information must be available to the Player in his Electronic Account.

The License Holder must, following the Player’s request, provide a statement of the Electronic Account for every transaction made during the last twelve (12) months.

**Note:** We can provide Playcheck data for gaming history. The operator will need to provide the additional data mentioned. Statement requests from the player will be operator responsibility. The default .com PlayCheck config should suffice

## Incomplete Games

A mechanism in order to complete an incomplete game must be provided to the player.

If a player has an incomplete game play, the interactive gaming system shall display the incomplete game to be completed upon reconnection or each time a new player session is activated

Bets related to incomplete game plays that can be continued, must be held by the interactive gaming system until the game is completed. The players’ accounts must display any funds kept by incomplete games.

## Incomplete Games Sweeper

The game rules and/or the terms and conditions must clearly determine what happens to the bets when the game remains without an outcome after the specified period, and the interactive gaming system must be able to refund or forfeit the bets.

If a game cannot be continued due to an action of the interactive gaming system, all bets must be refunded to the players of said game.

**Note:** We can use the default of 90 days to set up the incomplete games sweeper job. This must be mentioned in the T&C and/or game rules.

## Integrity Checker ?

Interactive Gaming Systems must be able to certify that every component of the control program contained in the Interactive Gaming System is an authentic copy of the authorized components of the Interactive Gaming System.

This process must be done automatically during its installation and at least every 24 hours, as well as following any such claim, using a method authorized by the regulatory body.

The authentication mechanism must use a hashing algorithm which produces a file of at least 128bits.

A recording or reporting system must be kept and be accessible for a period of 90 days, during which the identity verification results be displayed in detail for every control element.

## Responsible Gaming

Online gaming activities will be subject to a comprehensive social responsibility policy. Such policy shall combine preventive actions aimed at raising awareness and disseminating good gaming practices, as well as the potential effects of improper practices and their consequences.

Gaming operators shall have the obligation to:

1) Enable a function that allows players to set loss and session time limits;

2) Enable a function that allows players to apply for a temporary or permanent voluntary exclusion from the game and

3) Display to the player information about the last time he logged in from the moment that a Player logs into the system.

The License Holder must notify the player connected to the website by a message displayed on the website as soon as the latter exceeds the % of the limit he has set from time to time, whether time or money limit.

The message cannot be displayed for a period less than fifteen (15) seconds.

### Loss Limits

The player must be able to set limits in respect to his losses and the time of participation in the games on a daily, weekly and monthly basis.

Following registration and upon starting their first Playing Session, it is mandatory for the Player to set a Daily Loss and a Session Loss in order to participate in the Game

### Session Limits

Following registration and upon starting their first Playing Session, it is mandatory for the Player to set the total maximum time of participation in games on a daily basis, with the potential to set a total maximum time of participation in the Games on a daily basis, on a weekly basis and/or on a monthly basis)

## Self-Exclusions

The License Holder must allow the player to ban himself, via the Website, provisionally or permanently from participating in the games.

Players cannot participate in the games after they self-ban.

Players must be provided with the option of provisional abstention from participation in the Games, for twenty-four (24) hours.

The player cannot be re-registered before one (1) year has passed since their account was closed due to a permanent self-ban

A mechanism that allows players to cancel a self-ban is recommended.

## External Register

Before creating an Electronic Account, the License Holder must consult with the **Banned Persons Register** in order to ascertain that the person who wishes to create an Electronic Account is not registered there.

Registering a Player in the **Banned Persons Register** means that said Player is excluded from Participating in the Games of every Holder. Every License Holder ensures that any Player registered in the Register cannot Participate for as long as they are registered therein.

## Session Inactivity

If the gaming platform does not receive a response for the player device for 30 minutes or for the period specified by the regulatory authority, it must send a command that the time limit has expired due to the user’s inactivity and terminate the session. A message must be displayed to the player that the time limit has expired due to the player’s inactivity.

## Certification

Games must be certified.

The **RNG** must be certified.

The **SAFE** system must be certified.

## SAFE

The **Safe** shall be in a Data Center inside the Greek State. The alternative storage location of the main **Safe** shall be in a Date Center inside the EU or the EEA, at a distance of at least 30 kilometers from the main **Safe** and shall be synced to it every 30 minutes.

Maximum delay between each event and the time it entered the **Safe** is two (2) hours. Therefore, data shall be transferred to the **Safe** every two (2) hours at the latest.

The safe must be certified by an international body.

Data must be retained for at least ten (10) years.

## Reporting

There are numerous reporting requirements listed in the regulations. These include a:

- Player Session Report
- Gaming Performance Report
- Gaming Revenue Report
- Progressive Jackpot Configuration Report
- Progressive Jackpot Won Report
- Significant Event Report
- Change Notification Report
- Exclusions Report
- Account Balance Adjustment Report
- Promotional Account Summary Report
- Operator Revenue Report
- Operator Liability Report
- Summary Promotion/Bonus Report
- Upcoming Events Report
- Tournament Report

**Note:** It is operator responsibility to provide these reports. We will support them with gaming data for our games where applicable.
